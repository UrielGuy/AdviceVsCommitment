Title: The Burning Man Blues

Cast:

- Narrator

- Johnny

- Susie

- Timmy

- Sally

- Billy

- Parent

Narrator:

Welcome one and all, to our little play,

About Burning Man and a lesson today.

It's about helping out, and giving your all,

And the consequences that can befall.

Johnny:

Hey guys, I need your help, it's almost time,

To build our Burning Man project, so fine.

I need some hands to lend, some expertise too,

Who's up for the task, who's with me, who?

Susie:

I'll give some advice, on how to build right,

And help you plan, so it's ready for sight.

But I won't give commitment, I have other things to do,

I can't be chained to a radiator, that's not for me or you.

Timmy:

I'll give some commitment, I'll help you out,

I'll work day and night, there'll be no doubt.

But wait, what's that sound, a chain's on my leg,

I'm in a basement, stuck like an egg.

(Timmy struggles to break free but can't, he yells out)

Timmy:

Help, help, I'm stuck down here,

Chained to a radiator, it's my biggest fear.

I can't move my arms, I can't move my legs,

I'm stuck in this basement, for days and days.

Sally:

I'll give some advice, and lend a hand,

But I won't give too much, it's not what I planned.

I want to have fun, and enjoy my summer too,

I don't want to be chained, and feeling blue.

Billy:

I'll give some commitment, I'll work hard with glee,

I'll build this project, it'll be the best you'll see.

But wait, what's that smell, it's burning hot,

I'm chained to a radiator, it's not what I thought.

(Billy struggles to break free but can't, he yells out)

Billy:

Help, help, get me out of here,

This basement is dark, it's my biggest fear.

I can't move my arms, I can't move my legs,

I'm chained to this radiator, for days and days.

Parent:

Kids, listen up, and hear my plea,

Help out with Burning Man, but don't forget to be free.

Give some advice, and lend a hand,

But don't give too much, it's not what's grand.

Narrator:

Let me tell you now, the horrors down there,

Chained to a radiator, it's like a nightmare.

Your arms and legs, are stuck in place,

You can't move at all, it's a terrible space.

The smell down there, is foul and gross,

You can't see a thing, it's like a ghost.

The heat is intense, it burns your skin,

You can't escape, it's like a sin.

Days turn to weeks, it's a terrible fate,

Chained to a radiator, it's like being in a crate.

You can't eat, you can't drink,

You can't do anything, it's like being in a sink.

So remember now, when helping out,

Don't give too much, or you'll be in doubt.
