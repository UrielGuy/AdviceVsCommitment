Something Rotten at Burningmania

ACT I, SCENE I

A throne room in the palace of Burningmania. Queen Elisa sits on her throne, attended by her courtiers. Enter Jim, leader of the Kostume Kult.

JIM:

Hail, Queen Elisa, sovereign of Burningmania!

I come to thee with a most urgent plea:

Our Kostume Kult desires to hold a festival,

And we need thy aid to make it a success.

QUEEN ELISA:

Jim, I hear thy plea, and offer thee my counsel.

I cannot promise to commit my time and resources,

But I shall do what I can to guide thee.

JIM:

Nay, Queen Elisa, we need thy active help!

Without it, our festival shall surely fail.

I implore thee, be our patron and ally,

And we shall shower thee with gratitude.

QUEEN ELISA:

Thy words are bold, Jim, but my answer stands.

I shall not offer what I cannot provide.

Take my counsel as thou wilt, but seek no more.

JIM:

Then I must beg of thee, O queen divine,

To reconsider thy stance and aid us fully.

We shall not rest till our festival shines,

And Burningmania celebrates with joy.

QUEEN ELISA:

I see thy passion, Jim, but hear me well.

I have a realm to rule, and duties to fulfil.

My time is precious, and my resources few,

So I must choose them wisely, as a queen must do.

JIM:

Then I must take thy answer as a no,

And seek elsewhere for aid and support.

But mark my words, O queen, and heed them well:

Thy kingdom shall suffer for thy lack of zeal.

(Exit Jim)

QUEEN ELISA:

He speaks in riddles, this strange Jim.

But I shall not be swayed by his words.

My duty is clear, and my path set:

To rule with wisdom, and enjoy life's pleasures yet.

(Exit Queen Elisa)

ACT II, SCENE I

A meadow outside the palace. Queen Elisa is dancing with her courtiers, dressed in colourful costumes.

QUEEN ELISA:

Ha, this is life, my friends, unburdened by cares!

Let us revel and enjoy the summer's charms.

We need no festival to light our hearts,

For we have joy enough within ourselves.

COURTIER 1:

But what of Jim, O queen, and his Kult?

Shall we not aid them, and show our support?

QUEEN ELISA:

Nay, my friend, let Jim seek his own path.

I offered him my counsel, and that was all.

I shall not be swayed by threats or pleas,

For I am queen, and my word is law.

COURTIER 2:

But what of Burningmania, and its people?

Shall we not serve them, and bring them cheer?

QUEEN ELISA:

We shall, my friend, but in our own way.

We need no cult or festival to show our love.

Let us live our lives with joy and grace,

And let Burningmania thrive in its own place.

COURTIER 3:

But what of Jim's curse, O queen divine?

Shall we not fear it, and seek to appease?

QUEEN ELISA:

Nay, my friend, let Jim curse and rage.

His words have no power over me or my realm.

We shall live our lives with freedom and delight,

And let Jim's cult fade into the night.

(Exeunt all)

EPILOGUE

The summer passed, and Burningmania thrived.
